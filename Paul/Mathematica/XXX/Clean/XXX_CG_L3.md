## Overview

This is the second Clean notebook in the XXX subproject: the three-site ($L=3$) rational
$\mathfrak{gl}(2)$ (XXX) spin chain — independent representations $[\lambda_1,0]$, $[\lambda_2,0]$,
$[\lambda_3,0]$ at each site, independent inhomogeneities $\theta_1,\theta_2,\theta_3$, and a
shared companion twist $G$ — generalized directly from `XXX_CG_L2_Clean.wb`, plus the associated
Baxter TQ-equation solver. Unlike L2, this notebook was built **directly in `Clean/`** cell by cell
(no `Experiments/` merge/promotion step): it carries its own complete validation suite, including
the end-to-end TQ residual check that at L2 only lives in the separate
`Experiments/Baxter_Solver_L2.wb`. It is a 44-cell notebook, structured in two sections mirroring
L2's own layout:

- **Section A** (Cells 1–32) — numeric parameters, GT-basis/generator machinery (ported verbatim
  from L2, itself ported from `Experiments/XXX_su2.wb`, single-site and $L$-independent),
  three-site Lax/monodromy/transfer matrices, the fusion (Hirota) hierarchy, the SoV $B$-operator,
  and the SoV pseudovacuum/covector basis — each followed by its own inline validation cell, same
  convention as L2.
- **Section B** (Cells 33–44) — the Baxter/TQ solver: Baxter-equation ingredients, transfer-matrix
  coefficient extraction, the predicted weight-subspace dimension, the genuine
  transfer-matrix-eigenstate solve (`TauEigensystem`), accessors (`Psi`/`τ`), the linear TQ solve
  (`QSolve`/`Q1`), and the end-to-end TQ-equation residual regression check.

Every construction in Section A that has a direct $L=1$/$L=2$ counterpart is cross-referenced
against `paper/xxxCG.tex` §2 below, generalized from the $L=1$ formulas the same way L2's were —
these are tensor-product generalizations, not literal transcriptions, since the paper doesn't state
an $L=2$ or $L=3$ case explicitly. Section B has no paper counterpart at all — `paper/xxxCG.tex`
stops at the SoV pseudovacuum/covector construction and does not yet state a TQ equation.

**Structural difference from L2 (the reason this notebook needed real re-derivation, not just
mechanical generalization):** at $L=2$ the transfer matrix $t_{1,1}(u)$ is quadratic in $u$, with
one genuinely state-dependent expansion coefficient ($C_1$, feeding a single free number $J$ per
state) besides the trivial leading term. At $L=3$, $t_{1,1}(u)$ is **cubic**: $C_3\propto\mathbb{1}$
(trivial), $C_2$ is a pure Cartan/total-generator quantity (verified numerically in Cell 23 below —
this is the load-bearing cross-check the whole Baxter re-derivation depends on), but $C_1$ and $C_0$
genuinely mix different sites' raising/lowering operators and do **not** reduce to closed forms —
they are recorded directly per eigenvector as **two** free numbers $(J_1,J_0)$ (L2's single $J$
generalizes to a pair). This changes `TauEigensystem`, `BaxterMatrix`, and `QSolve` relative to
their L2 counterparts in ways spelled out symbol-by-symbol below; everything else in Section A
(fusion hierarchy, `dPred`, `Q1`) generalizes mechanically since the algebraic form doesn't
reference $L$ explicitly.

## Symbol reference — Section B (Baxter/TQ solver)

The routines below jointly solve $t_{1,1}(u)\Psi=\tau(u)\Psi$ and the Baxter TQ equation
$$\kappa_1\,a(u)\,q(u+h) + \kappa_2\,Q_\theta(u)\,q(u-h) = \tau(u)\,q(u)$$
for the three-site chain, following the same two-stage strategy as L2 (`TauEigensystem` diagonalizes
the genuine transfer matrix directly, then `QSolve` linearly solves for $q_n$ at the now-known
eigenvalue data) but with **two** free per-state numbers $(J_1,J_0)$ instead of L2's single $J$,
because $t_{1,1}(u)$ is cubic rather than quadratic (see Overview). `paper/xxxCG.tex` has no TQ
equation yet, so none of Section B has a paper cross-reference.

### `aFun[λ1,λ2,λ3][u]`, `τ0[λ1,λ2,λ3,M][u]`, `qpoly[clist,uu]`

- **Purpose:** the Baxter-equation ingredients: the Bethe/Baxter "$a(u)$" coefficient, the
  state-independent part of the transfer-matrix eigenvalue $\tau_0$, and a helper that evaluates an
  ascending-coefficient-list polynomial without an indeterminate $0^0$. Unlike L2, `Qθ` is **not**
  redefined here — Section B reuses the single `Qθ` from Cell 26 (Section A) directly, since this
  notebook was never split into two separately self-contained source files (see the `Qθ` gotcha
  above).
- **Signature:**
  - `aFun[λ1_,λ2_,λ3_][u_] := (u-θ1-h λ1)(u-θ2-h λ2)(u-θ3-h λ3)`.
  - `τ0[λ1_,λ2_,λ3_,M_][u_] := (κ1+κ2)u^3 - (θ1+θ2+θ3)(κ1+κ2)u^2 - u^2 h(κ1(λ1+λ2+λ3-M)+κ2 M)` —
    note **only** $u^3,u^2$ terms (zero $u^1,u^0$ coefficients by construction): the full eigenvalue
    is $\tau=\tau_0+J_1u+J_0$ with $(J_1,J_0)$ the two free per-state numbers.
  - `qpoly[clist_,uu_] := Module[{x}, (clist.x^Range[0,Length[clist]-1]) /. x -> uu]` — identical to
    L2's.
- **Defined in:** Cell 34.
- **Paper reference:** none — `paper/xxxCG.tex` does not yet state a Baxter/TQ equation. Standard
  XXX Baxter-equation form, $a(u)=\prod_i(u-\theta_i-h\lambda_i)$, $Q_\theta(u)=\prod_i(u-\theta_i)$,
  generalized to three inhomogeneous sites with a companion twist of eigenvalues $\kappa_1,\kappa_2$.
- **Example** (Cell 34):
  ```wolfram
  {aFun[1, 1, 1][u], Qθ[u], τ0[1, 1, 1, 1][u]}
  (* {(u-9/5)(u-4/3)(u-8/7), (u-4/5)(u-1/3)(u-1/7),
      (0.720879750294453074533355301191+0.×10^-30 I) u^3
      - (2.00129949724602924977593400283+0.93278244859337471648483598207 I) u^2} *)
  ```
  confirming `τ0[1,1,1,1][u]` indeed has only $u^3,u^2$ terms.

### `BaxterMatrix[λ1,λ2,λ3,M]`

- **Purpose:** the fixed linear operator, in the monic-degree-$M$-polynomial coefficient basis,
  whose nullspace at known $(J_1,J_0)$ gives the Q-function's coefficients.
- **Signature:** `BaxterMatrix[λ1_,λ2_,λ3_,M_]` builds, for $k=0,\dots,M$, the column
  $O[u^k] = \kappa_1\,a(u)(u+h)^k + \kappa_2\,Q_\theta(u)(u-h)^k - \tau_0(u)\,u^k$ (now degree $k+3$
  since $a,Q_\theta,\tau_0$ are cubic, one degree higher than L2's quadratic case), expands it as a
  polynomial in $u$, and hard-asserts (`Abort[]` on failure) that the degree-$(M{+}2)$ and
  $(M{+}3)$ coefficients vanish for every column. The surviving degree-$0..M$ coefficients become
  the matrix's columns.
- **Returns:** an $(M{+}1)\times(M{+}1)$ matrix.
- **Depends on:** [`aFun`, `τ0`](#afunλ1λ2λ3u-τ0λ1λ2λ3mu-qpolyclistuu), Section A's [`Qθ`](#qθu-section-a).
- **Defined in:** Cell 35.
- **L2 comparison (important structural difference, not a bug):** L2's `BaxterMatrix` hard-asserted
  the top **two** extra-degree coefficients, $M{+}1$ and $M{+}2$. Here, the hard assert checks
  exactly the top two extra-degree coefficients again — but they are $M{+}2$ and $M{+}3$, **not**
  $M{+}1$. Degree $M{+}1$ is **deliberately not checked** here: writing $O_0[u^k]$ (the
  $\tau_0$-only construction used by this routine) versus the true $O_{\mathrm{full}}[u^k]$ (built
  from the full $\tau=\tau_0+J_1u+J_0$), the relation is
  $O_0[u^k]=O_{\mathrm{full}}[u^k]+J_1u^{k+1}+J_0u^k$. The $J_0$ term contaminates the diagonal
  (rows $0..M$, undone later by `QSolve`'s $J_0\cdot\mathbb{1}$ subtraction); the $J_1$ term
  contaminates row $k{+}1$, which for the top column $k=M$ reaches exactly row $M{+}1$ — a row that
  is genuinely **not** a pure per-column identity at $L=3$ (unlike $L=2$, which has no $J_1$-like
  free parameter feeding an off-diagonal contamination). Row $M{+}1$'s physical content is exactly
  what `QSolve`'s $J_1\cdot(\text{shiftDown})$ correction supplies later; it is validated not here
  but by the end-to-end TQ residual check (Cell 43/44). Rows $M{+}2,M{+}3$ are never reached by
  either contamination and stay clean per-column identities — hence exactly two checks, same count
  as L2, just shifted up one degree.
- **Verified by:** the hard in-function assert; Cell 35's smoke test
  `Dimensions /@ (BaxterMatrix[1,1,1,#] & /@ {0,1,2,3})` gives `{{1,1},{2,2},{3,3},{4,4}}`, no
  assertion failure printed.
- **Example** (Cell 35):
  ```wolfram
  Dimensions /@ (BaxterMatrix[1, 1, 1, #] & /@ {0, 1, 2, 3})
  (* {{1,1},{2,2},{3,3},{4,4}} *)
  ```

  3: `{C0,C1,C2}`).
- **Signature:** `t3Coeffs[λ1_,λ2_,λ3_] := t3Coeffs[λ1,λ2,λ3] = Module[...]` (memoized).
- **Depends on:** Section A's [`t3[·][1,1][·]`](#t3λ1λ2λ311u).

- **Verified by:** Cell 36's own check, `Max[Abs[Flatten[Cs[[4]] - (κ1+κ2) IdentityMatrix[8]]]]`

### `dPred[M,λ1,λ2,λ3]`

- **Purpose:** predicted dimension of the weight-$M$ subspace of
  $V_{\lambda_1}\otimes V_{\lambda_2}\otimes V_{\lambda_3}$ — the number of $(m_1,m_2,m_3)$ triples
  with $m_1+m_2+m_3=M$ fixed and $0\le m_i\le\lambda_i$. This is the exact multiplicity
  `TauEigensystem` must reproduce per $M$-sector.
- **Signature:** `dPred[M_,λ1_,λ2_,λ3_] := Sum[If[0<=M-m1-m2<=λ3,1,0],{m1,0,λ1},{m2,0,λ2}]` — a
  genuine **triple convolution** (double explicit sum plus the implicit third-site bound), unlike
  L2's closed form `Min[M,λ1,λ2,λ1+λ2-M]+1`. There is no closed form at $L=3$; small ranges make
  the brute double-sum cheap.
- **Defined in:** Cell 37.
- **Depends on:** nothing (pure combinatorics).
- **Gotcha (propagates to every accessor below):** because `dPred` at $L=3$ can exceed $M{+}1$
  (e.g. `dPred[1,1,1,1]=3 > M+1=2` — a binomial-like distribution across three sites), the
  gross-bounds sanity check on the index `n` in `Psi`/`τ`/`QSolve` below only requires `n>=0`, **not**
  `n<=M` — L2's accessors included an `n<=M` gross check that was always true-but-redundant there
  (since L2's `dPred=Min[M,λ1,λ2,λ1+λ2-M]+1<=M+1` by construction, $M$ being one of `Min`'s own
  arguments), but at L3 an `n<=M` check would wrongly reject some genuine states before the real
  multiplicity check (`n>mult-1`) ever runs.
- **Verified by:** Cell 37, `{Sum[dPred[M,1,1,1],{M,0,3}], dim[{1,0}]dim[{1,0}]dim[{1,0}]} -> {8,8}`
  — confirms the weight-sector counts sum to the total representation dimension.

### `TauEigensystem[λ1,λ2,λ3]`

- **Purpose:** diagonalizes the *genuine* transfer matrix $t_{1,1}[\lambda_1,\lambda_2,\lambda_3](u)$
  directly, jointly extracting the weight label $M$ (read algebraically) and two free per-state
  numbers $(J_1,J_0)$ (read directly, no formula) for every eigenstate, with a hard genuineness
  residual check.
- **Signature:** `TauEigensystem[λ1_,λ2_,λ3_] := TauEigensystem[λ1,λ2,λ3] = Module[...]` (memoized).
  Algorithm: since $C_0,C_1,C_2,C_3$ pairwise commute (and $C_3$ is already known to be scalar),
  diagonalizes a generic **two**-parameter combination $C_0+r_1C_1+r_2C_2$ (L2 needed only one
  parameter $r$), trying six candidate $(r_1,r_2)$ pairs (e.g. `{7/3,11/5}`, `{π,e}`, ...) until the
  combination's eigenvectors have full rank $d=(\lambda_1{+}1)(\lambda_2{+}1)(\lambda_3{+}1)$ and $d$
  distinct eigenvalues. For each eigenvector $v$: extracts $t_0=\frac{v\cdot C_0v}{v\cdot v}$,
  $t_1=\frac{v\cdot C_1v}{v\cdot v}$, $t_2=\frac{v\cdot C_2v}{v\cdot v}$, and hard-`Abort`s if any of
  the three residuals $\|C_iv-t_iv\|$ ($i=0,1,2$) exceeds $10^{-15}$. $M$ is read off algebraically
  from $t_2$ (the L3 analog of L2 reading $M$ from $t_1$, one order down since the polynomial is one
  degree higher) via
  $$M=\frac{-(\theta_1+\theta_2+\theta_3)(\kappa_1+\kappa_2)-h\kappa_1(\lambda_1+\lambda_2+\lambda_3)-t_2}{h(\kappa_2-\kappa_1)}$$
  hard-asserted within $10^{-10}$ of an integer; $(J_1,J_0)=(t_1,t_0)$ are recorded **directly per
  eigenvector** with no closed-form formula (unlike L2, which likewise recorded $J=t_0$ directly,
  but only needed the one number). States within each $M$-sector are sorted ascending by
  $(\mathrm{Re}[J_0],\mathrm{Im}[J_0],\mathrm{Re}[J_1],\mathrm{Im}[J_1])$ to assign $n$
  (L2 sorted by $(\mathrm{Re}[J],\mathrm{Im}[J])$ only, since it had a single free number), and each
  sector's count is hard-asserted to equal `dPred[M,λ1,λ2,λ3]` exactly.
- **Returns:** an `Association` keyed by `{M,n}` $\to$ `<|"J1"->J1_n, "J0"->J0_n, "Psi"->eigenvector normalized to last component 1|>`
  (L2's returned `<|"J"->..., "Psi"->...|>` had one fewer key).
- **Depends on:** [`t3Coeffs`](#t3coeffsλ1λ2λ3), [`dPred`](#dpredmλ1λ2λ3).
- **Defined in:** Cell 38.
- **Paper reference:** none (Baxter/TQ machinery not yet in `paper/xxxCG.tex`).
- **Verified by:** the in-function hard asserts (rank/degeneracy resolution over the two-parameter
  combination, residual $<10^{-15}$ against all three of $C_0,C_1,C_2$, integer $M$ within
  $10^{-10}$, per-sector count exactly matching `dPred`) constitute the check; a real failure
  aborts rather than silently returning wrong data. The smoke test in Cell 38 evaluates
  `KeySort[TauEigensystem[1,1,1]][[All,"J0"]] // N` for all 8 states with no error, and the notebook
  additionally carries the independent end-to-end TQ residual check (Cell 43/44 below), which
  re-confirms `TauEigensystem`'s output from a completely different derivation path (through
  `QSolve`/`BaxterMatrix` rather than the $C_0,C_1,C_2$ extraction used internally here).
- **Example** (Cell 38):
  ```wolfram
  KeySort[TauEigensystem[1, 1, 1]][[All, "J0"]] // N
  (* <|{0,0}->-1.0023661289808585-2.5229544323858897 I, {1,0}->-1.9591204341670663-0.8728625487854049 I,
       {1,1}->-0.08422543749008554+0.27639976349988676 I, {1,2}->1.0375469819606056-0.9937091985069969 I,
       {2,0}->-1.9591204341670663+0.8728625487854049 I, {2,1}->-0.08422543749008554-0.27639976349988676 I,
       {2,2}->1.0375469819606056+0.9937091985069969 I, {3,0}->-1.0023661289808585+2.5229544323858897 I|> *)
  ```
  Note the $M=1$ and $M=2$ sectors each carry **three** states ($n=0,1,2$) — `dPred[1,1,1,1]=3`,
  exceeding $M{+}1=2$, a concrete illustration of the "gotcha" recorded under `dPred` above.

### `Psi[λ1,λ2,λ3][M,n]`, `τ[λ1,λ2,λ3][M,n][u]`

- **Purpose:** bounds-checked, on-demand accessors over the shared `TauEigensystem` cache. `Psi`
  returns the fixed (last-component-normalized) eigenvector; `τ` returns the full eigenvalue
  polynomial $\tau_0(u)+J_{1,n}u+J_{0,n}$ (L2's accessor returned $\tau_0(u)+J_n$, one fewer term).
- **Signature:** `Psi[λ1_,λ2_,λ3_][M_,n_]`, `τ[λ1_,λ2_,λ3_][M_,n_][u_]`. Both require $M$ an integer
  in $0..\lambda_1+\lambda_2+\lambda_3$ and $n$ a non-negative integer (else `Message[·::bnds]` and
  `$Failed`) — deliberately **no** `n<=M` gross bound, per the `dPred` gotcha above; if $n$ passes
  the gross check but exceeds the actual weight multiplicity `dPred[M,λ1,λ2,λ3]-1`, fires
  `Message[·::spurious]` and returns `Missing["NoState",...]`.
- **Depends on:** [`TauEigensystem`](#taueigensystemλ1λ2λ3), [`dPred`](#dpredmλ1λ2λ3),
  [`τ0`](#afunλ1λ2λ3u-τ0λ1λ2λ3mu-qpolyclistuu).
- **Defined in:** Cell 39.
- **Verified by:** the smoke test in Cell 39 deliberately calls `Psi[2,1,1][3,3]` — `dPred[3,2,1,1]=3`,
  so only $n=0,1,2$ actually exist even though $n=3\le M=3$ passes the gross bound — and confirms
  the expected `Psi::spurious` message and `Missing[...]` result:
  ```
  Psi::spurious: No genuine state at (M,n)=(3,3) for (λ1,λ2,λ3)=(2,1,1): weight sector M=3 has only 3 genuine state(s) (n=0..2), so n=3 does not exist.
  ```
- **Example** (Cell 39, valid case):
  ```wolfram
  {Psi[1, 1, 1][1, 0], N[τ[1, 1, 1][1, 0][2], 10]}
  (* {{0.360439875147226537266677651+0.932782448593374716484835982 I, ..., 1.00000000000000000000000000+0.×10^-27 I},
      2.455604872 - 1.170833864 I} *)
  ```

### `QSolve[λ1,λ2,λ3][M,n]`

- **Purpose:** solves the Baxter TQ equation as a *linear* problem (both $J_1$ and $J_0$, hence
  $\tau$, are already known from `TauEigensystem`): finds the monic degree-$M$ polynomial $q_n(u)$
  with `(BaxterMatrix[λ1,λ2,λ3,M] - J1n·shiftDown - J0n·Id).q_n = 0`.
- **Signature:** `QSolve[λ1_,λ2_,λ3_][M_,n_] := QSolve[λ1,λ2,λ3][M,n] = Module[...]` (memoized). Same
  bounds/spurious/gross-check style as `Psi`/`τ` (`n>=0` only, no `n<=M`). Builds
  `shiftDown = Table[If[i==j+1,1,0],{i,1,M+1},{j,1,M+1}]` — the $(M{+}1)\times(M{+}1)$ matrix with
  1's on the subdiagonal (row $=$ col $+1$) — representing multiplication of the underlying $u^k$
  monomial by an extra $u^1$, i.e. "$J_1u^{k+1}$" landing one row below the diagonal relative to
  column $k$. Subtracts **both** `J1n shiftDown` and `J0n IdentityMatrix[M+1]` from `BaxterMatrix`
  before the nullspace solve — L2 only needed a single `J·IdentityMatrix` subtraction, since L2's
  $\tau$ had only the one free constant term.
- **Returns:** the length-$(M{+}1)$ nullspace vector normalized so its last (top, $u^M$) coefficient
  is $1$ (monic), or `$Failed`/message on `degnull`/`lastzero`/`bnds`/`spurious` failure.
- **Depends on:** [`BaxterMatrix`](#baxtermatrixλ1λ2λ3m), [`TauEigensystem`](#taueigensystemλ1λ2λ3),
  [`dPred`](#dpredmλ1λ2λ3).
- **Defined in:** Cell 40.
- **Precision gotcha (inherited unchanged from L2):** the routine's own comment records the same
  precision-tracked-exact-zero issue as L2's `QSolve` — the would-be-zero entries of
  `BaxterMatrix - J1n·shiftDown - J0n·Id` are singular by construction but come out as
  precision-tracked (not confirmed) zeros, which a bare `NullSpace` call can treat as unknown rather
  than zero. The fix is identical to L2's: `Chop` the matrix at threshold $10^{-12}$ before
  `NullSpace`, with an additional explicit `Tolerance -> 10^-10` kept for headroom.
- **Verified by:** no standalone regression-guard cell in this notebook (unlike L2, where a specific
  historically-critical case was called out in `Baxter_Solver_L2.wb`); Cell 40's own smoke test
  `QSolve[1,1,1][2,0]` runs cleanly with no error, and end-to-end correctness of the resulting
  $q_n$ across every $(M,n)$ is confirmed by the Cell 43/44 residual check below.
- **Example** (Cell 40):
  ```wolfram
  QSolve[1, 1, 1][2, 0]
  (* {-0.04995738064123986793660273-1.230053239147504224150097 I,
      -1.856051357929965424850272+1.317538826427866191621732 I,
      1.0000000000000000000000000+0.×10^-26 I} *)
  ```

### `checkTQ[λ1,λ2,λ3,M,n]` and the end-to-end TQ residual regression check

- **Purpose:** the notebook's own complete end-to-end validation of the Baxter TQ equation, built
  entirely from `Q1` and `τ` — two quantities that trace back to genuinely different derivation
  paths (`Q1` through `QSolve`/`BaxterMatrix`; `τ` through `TauEigensystem`'s $C_0,C_1,C_2$
  diagonalization), so a small residual is strong evidence that both derivations, including the
  re-derived L3 $M$-extraction/$(J_1,J_0)$ structure, are correct. **At L2 this check lived only in
  the separate `Experiments/Baxter_Solver_L2.wb`; here it is a first-class part of the Clean
  notebook itself**, per this notebook's "no dropped validation" design constraint.
- **Signature:** `checkTQ[λ1_,λ2_,λ3_,M_,n_] := Max[Abs[Table[aFun[λ1,λ2,λ3][uu] Q1[λ1,λ2,λ3][M,n][uu+h] - τ[λ1,λ2,λ3][M,n][uu] Q1[λ1,λ2,λ3][M,n][uu] + Qθ[uu] Q1[λ1,λ2,λ3][M,n][uu-h], {uu,{0,1,2,-1,5}}]]]` —
  the residual of
  $$a(u)\,Q(u+h) - \tau(u)\,Q(u) + Q_\theta(u)\,Q(u-h) = 0$$
  at five sample points avoiding roots of $a$ and $Q_\theta$.
- **Defined in:** Cell 43.
- **Gotcha (deliberate, commented in the notebook):** the outer sampling loop uses `uu`, not `u`, as
  its `Table` iteration variable. `BaxterMatrix` (Cell 35) uses a bare, non-`Module`-localized `u`
  internally for `CoefficientList`; if the outer loop used the same bare symbol `u`, `Table`'s
  dynamic (`Block`-style) binding could clobber `BaxterMatrix`'s internal `u` the first time an
  uncached `QSolve` call triggers a fresh `BaxterMatrix` evaluation while `u` happens to be
  numerically bound. This is the same convention the L2 predecessor
  (`Experiments/Baxter_Solver_L2.wb`) already used for exactly this reason.
- **Verified by:** Cell 43 checks every $(M,n)$ at $(\lambda_1,\lambda_2,\lambda_3)=(1,1,1)$: all 8
  residuals land between $\sim10^{-19}$ and $\sim10^{-23}$. Cell 44 checks the asymmetric case
  $(\lambda_1,\lambda_2,\lambda_3)=(2,1,1)$: all 12 residuals land between $\sim10^{-18}$ and
  $\sim10^{-23}$. Both are exact zero at the tracked ~30-digit numeric precision budget — matching
  the plan's stated result ("TQ residual check exact zero at 18–23 tracked digits for both $(1,1,1)$
  and $(2,1,1)$").
- **Example** (Cell 43 output, $(\lambda_1,\lambda_2,\lambda_3)=(1,1,1)$):
  ```wolfram

      {2,0,0.×10^-21}, {2,1,0.×10^-21}, {2,2,0.×10^-21}, {3,0,0.×10^-19}} *)
  ```

- **Example** (Cell 44 output, $(\lambda_1,\lambda_2,\lambda_3)=(2,1,1)$):

- **`κ[1]`/`κ[2]` vs `κ1`/`κ2`:** Cell 19's `t3diag` and Cell 22's `t3diagasympt` use the formal,
  purely symbolic indexed function `κ[i]` — unrelated to the numeric plain variables `κ1`,`κ2` set
  in Cell 1 (the actual companion-twist eigenvalues used everywhere else). They only appear
  together, symbolically, in the Cell 23 asymptotics cross-check — same convention as L2.
- **Symbolic `Simplify//Flatten//Union` checks don't literally collapse to `{0}` at L3.** Both the
  transfer-matrix commutativity check (Cell 17) and the SoV covector eigenvalue check (Cell 31) fail
  to simplify down to the literal singleton `{0}` the way their L2 counterparts did — the notebook's
  own comments (Cells 18 and 32) attribute this to mixed exact-rational and 30-digit-precision
  numeric arithmetic that `Simplify` doesn't always fully merge, not to an actual nonzero residual.
  Each is followed by a substantive numeric confirmation at a fixed generic complex point instead
  (Cells 18 and 32 respectively), which does `Chop` down to exactly `0`.
- **`dPred` can exceed $M+1$ — no `n<=M` gross bound anywhere in Section B.** Unlike L2 (where
  `dPred=Min[M,λ1,λ2,λ1+λ2-M]+1` is always $\le M+1$ by construction), L3's `dPred` is a genuine
  triple convolution that can exceed $M+1$ (e.g. `dPred[1,1,1,1]=3>M+1=2`). `Psi`, `τ`, and `QSolve`
  therefore only gross-check `n>=0`, relying entirely on the `n>mult-1` multiplicity check
  (`mult=dPred[...]`) as the sole source of truth for which `n` actually exist per sector.
- **`BaxterMatrix`'s hard assert checks $M{+}2,M{+}3$, not $M{+}1$.** Degree $M{+}1$ is genuinely
  state-dependent (contaminated by $J_1$) at L3 and must **not** be asserted to vanish inside
  `BaxterMatrix` — its correct handling is `QSolve`'s job (`J1n·shiftDown`), validated only by the
  end-to-end residual check (Cells 43–44), not by a symbolic assert. See the full explanation under
  `BaxterMatrix` above; this generalizes to any chain length $L$ — exactly the top **two**
  extra-degree coefficients ($M{+}(L{-}1)$ and $M{+}L$) are always pure per-column identities,
  regardless of $L$.
- **`QSolve` subtracts two terms, not one.** `J1n·shiftDown + J0n·Identity`, both subtracted from
  `BaxterMatrix[λ1,λ2,λ3,M]` before the nullspace solve — L2's `QSolve` only needed `J·Identity`.
  The `Chop`-before-`NullSpace` precision-tracked-zero fix (threshold $10^{-12}$, plus explicit
  `Tolerance->10^-10`) is otherwise unchanged from L2.
- **Memoization caching stale results:** several routines (`ps`, `dim`, `J0`/`Jp`/`Jm`/`JJ`, `T`,
  `t3[1,s]`, `x3`, `t3Coeffs`, `TauEigensystem`, `QSolve`) are self-memoizing
  (`f[x_]:=f[x]=...`). Per the project-wide convention (`CLAUDE.md`), after fixing any upstream
  definition, re-run the defining cell and everything downstream that used the stale cache.
- **$h=1$, high-precision twist, reused verbatim from L2:** Cell 1 sets `κ1 = N[Exp[I Zeta[3]], 30]`
  (30-digit precision, complex, on the unit circle) with `κ2=1/κ1=Conjugate[κ1]`, and reuses
  $\theta_1=1/3$, $\theta_2=1/7$ from L2 verbatim; only $\theta_3=4/5$ is new, chosen (and
  mechanically checked in Cell 2) so that $\theta_3-\theta_1$ and $\theta_3-\theta_2$ are not
  integers, avoiding an accidental Baxter-equation degeneracy. Numeric checks throughout rely on
  this tracked precision — residuals reported at $10^{-18}$ to $10^{-29}$ are relative to this
  ~30-digit budget, not machine precision — so don't casually substitute lower-precision numeric
  values without re-checking the hard tolerance thresholds ($10^{-15}$, $10^{-10}$, $10^{-18}$)
  scattered through Section B.
- **Compute-cost-driven range trimming vs. L2:** several Section A validation `Table`s deliberately
  use a smaller $\lambda$-range than their L2 counterparts (e.g. Cell 17/31 use $\lambda_i\in\{1,2\}$
  where L2 used $\{1,\dots,4\}$ or $\{1,2,3\}$) because the physical-space dimension grows as
  $(\lambda+1)^3$ at three sites rather than $(\lambda+1)^2$ at two — this is a deliberate
  compute-cost tradeoff recorded in the cells' own comments, not a reduction in what's checked
  (both endpoints of the trimmed range still cover the general/unequal-$\lambda$ case).
